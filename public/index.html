About Me

My name is Greg Mireles and I am originally from San Antonio, Texas. I am a U.S. Army Veteran with 10 years of Active-Duty service. In the Army, I was a Special Operations Communications Sergeant and a Telecommunications Operations Chief, which is where I developed and honed my technological skill set. I have had multiple combat deployments overseas, mainly Afghanistan, Iraq, and Africa. I exited the Military in December of 2013 and immediately moved to New York, where my new job was at with Harris Corporation, RF Division. While at Harris, I was a Technical Trainer 2, where I flew around the world training customers on Advanced Radio Communications. After a year in this capacity, I decided that I needed to attend college to finsih my undergraduate degree. At this point, I resigned from Harris and moved to Tacoma, WA to pursue my educational goals. In the summer of 2018, I graduated from The University of Washington Tacoma, with a Bachelor of Arts in Sustainable Urban Development, with a concentration in Geographic Information Systems. In the Fall of 2019 I started graduate school at UWT as a candidate of the MSc. in Geospatial Technologies program. Fast-forward a year later and I now have my MSc. in Geospatial Technologies and am currently working as a Software Development Engineer for Amazon Web Services (AWS).
Thank you for taking the time to read this and I hope you enjoy the site!
